Brooklyn Candidates For US House Racing To Fundraise The Most Before Primaries

Incumbents are significantly out-fundraising primary challengers so far in the race to represent Brooklyn in the US House of Representatives.

A street in Brooklyn. Photo by Ana Lucia Murillo for CUNY.

By Ana Lucia Murillo
Published March 20, 2020 2:00 PM

Fundraising is a crucial piece of running a successful campaign for political office. This year, Americans will be voting not only for their president but also for many other elected offices.

Significantly, we will be voting for our lawmakers from the House of Representatives. Before the general election in November comes the primary elections this summer.

According to data collection by the website FiveThirtyEight, the candidate who spends the most money in a race usually wins. More than 90% of candidates who spend the most on House seats end up winning, but that doesn’t necessarily mean that successful fundraising causes the win. Data collected by the website Open Secrets shows that incumbents typically raise much more money than primary challengers.

With that in mind, we wanted to look locally. How much have the various candidates for congress from Brooklyn raised as of March? Have incumbents raised more than primary challengers? I have pulled this data from the Federal Election Commission’s website.

Brooklyn is made up of six congressional districts- districts 7, 8, 9, 10, 11 and 12. There are 33 candidates running for six spots. Six of the top nine fundraisers so far are incumbents. And none of the bottom 24 fundraising spots are incumbents.

The only incumbents who are in the top nine fundraising spots are Nicole Malliotakis, who’s running to represent Staten Island and parts of Southern Brooklyn, and Lindsey Boylan and Holly Lynch, who would represent parts of lower Manhattan, as well as Borough Park and Bensonhurst in Brooklyn. Malliotakis received President Trump’s endorsement last month, who’s hoping she will take over the slot from Democrat Max Rose. She ran against Bill DeBlasio for mayor 2017 and lost.

Boylan decided to run against House Judiciary Committee chair Jerrold Nadler in order to fight against the Trump administration’s policies, according to an interview in Teen Vogue. Lynch is also running against Nadler, after trying to make the ballot two years ago but failing. Nadler has been in office since 1992.
